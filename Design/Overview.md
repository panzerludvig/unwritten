# Game Design Overview

## Concept
_What is Stories Yet Unwritten? One paragraph._ A tabletop-style RPG in the Dungeons & Dragons tradition — party, classes, dice, a world to explore — where the inhabitants are not scripted. Each NPC is simulated by a language model so that conversations are open-ended and characters remember, react, and pursue their own goals.

## Core Pillars
1. **Living NPCs** — characters are agents with memory, motive, and voice, not dialogue trees. See [[Design/NPCs]].
2. **Open conversation** — the player can say anything; the world responds in character. See [[Technical/NPC Simulation]].
3. **Mechanics still matter** — the LLM narrates and role-plays, but a real rules engine adjudicates outcomes. See [[Design/Mechanics]].

## Gameplay Loop
_Describe the moment-to-moment and session loop._ Explore → encounter a character or situation → converse or act freely → the rules engine resolves consequences → the world and its NPCs remember and change.
