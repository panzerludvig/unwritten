# Characters

Player characters — who they are, what they can do, and how they grow. The defining principle: a character is a **blank slate** shaped entirely by what the player does. For non-player characters see [[Design/NPCs]].

---

## Identity is earned, not chosen

Neither the player nor the DM declares who the character is at the start. There is no class selection and no DM-imposed backstory. The character begins undefined and *becomes* someone through their decisions and actions over the course of play. See [[Design/Core Concepts]] (You become what you do).

This is the player-character mirror of the whole project's "unwritten" premise — the character's story, like the world's, is written by playing.

---

## Action-driven progression

What a character does, they get better at. Identity and abilities emerge from a pattern of behaviour rather than from a chosen build:

- Fight often → grow more capable in combat.
- Sneak, steal, deceive → grow better at stealth and subterfuge.
- Act piously, show mercy, tend to others → may unlock blessings, healing, and other priestly/paladin-like abilities.

The familiar D&D archetypes (fighter, rogue, cleric, paladin…) are destinations a character can *arrive at* through how they play — not menus they pick from up front.

---

## Governing growth and abilities

There needs to be some system that tracks a character's accumulated behaviour and governs what abilities become available and when — so progression feels earned and consistent rather than arbitrary. The shape of this is **deliberately undecided**; it will be figured out as play reveals what's needed. See [[Technical/Current Stage]] and [[Design/Mechanics]].

Open questions to resolve through play:
- What exactly is tracked — broad tendencies, specific actions, thresholds?
- How does an ability "unlock," and is it visible to the player or discovered?
- How is this kept consistent in the narration-only stage, before any mechanical engine exists?
- Can a character drift — lose an aptitude they stop using, or shift identity over time?

---

## Character Creation

There is no creation step in the traditional sense. Play begins with a blank-slate character; see [[Design/Dungeon Master]] for how the opening scene starts without asserting who the player is.
